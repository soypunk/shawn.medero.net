---
title: "Problems With Apple Notes in iOS 9.0 and OS X 10.11"
date: "2015-09-10"
draft: false
---

When Apple demo'd the revamped Notes suite at WWDC 2015 I was very excited about the possibility of moving the majority of my note-taking into the native app. There's a lot of benefits of using native apps on iOS - primarily better integration with Spotlight, Siri, "Share Sheets", and platform features like "Slide Over."

After using the new Notes app for about two months I've concluded it is not really for me. The major issues I ran into are as follows:

- no control over the note list sort order
- no way to "pin a note" to the top of the note list
- alternatively, there's no way to mark a note as a "favorite"
- notes don't appear to have a URI scheme
- no easy way to enter checklist mode with the keyboard
- you can put notes into folders but there's no "all notes" view
- notes aren't integrated with Apple's "Label" (tagging) system

At this point I'm only reliably using Notes as a scratchpad but it is not useful to me as long-term storage or even week-to-week storage.

At the individual note level things are quite good. Checklist are functional enough and the markup tools are very good. The integration of Notes with Siri - particularly Reminders is great for things like "Remind me to review this note when I get home." I wish there was better keyboard access to the list, heading, and other styling functions. On the iPad Air 2 the ability to use Notes with the new "Slide Over" multi-tasking UI side-by-side with an app like Safari was super-handy. iCloud syncing was flawless and speedy. The biggest failings really are at the level of categorization, organization and referencing of notes.
