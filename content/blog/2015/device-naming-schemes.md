I realized today that I after I had rebuilt my MacBook Pro sometime after the release of Mac OS X Yosemite that I forget to change the computer name from the deafult "Shawn's MacBookPro". This is the default naming scheme in OS X (Generally: ``%FIRST_NAME%'s %SHORT_APPLE_MODEL_NAME%``) and it is fine and dandy although it expose some personally identifiable information that might not be ideal depending on your work or personal privacy preferences.

I've used any number of device naming schemes in the past for my personal devices (as well as from my time as a Systems Administrator) and as I was thinking up a new one I stumbled across quite a bit of existing thought around this topic:

* An entire [wiki of ready to use Naming Schemes][1]
* [Don Libes][3] wrote [RFC 1178: Choosing a Name for Your Computer][2]
* [Jay Ashworth][6] ([@_Baylink][7]) wrote [RFC 2100: The Naming of Hosts][5]
* ... and, of course, an [XKCD comic][4]

For a personal network, I have a nicely constrained problem space and not many odd requriements I'd have to work around. Personally, I settled on last names from [Wikipedia's _List of fictional butlers_][8] and may your own naming scheme choices be just as fanciful.
